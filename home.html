Stories and Myths

  Stories and myths are important because they help us make sense of the world and our place in it. Through narratives, we pass down shared values, explore universal truths, and connect with our history and culture.
  They give us a way to understand complex emotions, moral dilemmas, and the mysteries of life, often by personifying abstract concepts like love, fear, or fate. Myths, in particular, offer a lens through which we can examine the human experience across time and cultures, helping us see common threads that unite us all. Ultimately, stories and myths shape our identities and provide meaning in an often chaotic and unpredictable world.
